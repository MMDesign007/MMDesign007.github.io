What Is Pair Programming?

Pair programming is when two programmers work together to build a project. Programmers can switch between driver and navigator roles. The benefits of peer programming benefits the project by minimizing mistakes, and allows for programmers to learn from eachother.

Why is pair programming important to DBC?

Pair programming mimics the experience in the workplace, and allows programmers to learn from eachother.

Compare Driver and Navigator

The navigator gives direction to the driver, and the driver executes the plan.
