Thinking Style and Learning

October 26, 2014

According to the Gregorc Thinking style my approach to learning is Abstract Random. We were instructed to take the Gregorc Thinking Style assessment at the beginning of phase 0, and after 3 weeks at DBC I am realizing how accurate it really is for me. I do thrive in environments that are very people oriented, and I love working with others on projects, so the pair programming at DBC has definitely been good for me.

Another trait of abstract random thinkers is that they thrive in unstructured environments. I think that this is true for me, but I have also found that this part of Abstract Random thinking can be a bit distracting. I have had to re-wire a bit. Initially, I had issues with time management, but I found that I need structure to get through Dev Bootcamp phase 0. The Pomodoro technique, and time boxing, allows me to manage my time and stay on task. Moving throughout the program, I will continue to stay on top of time management to make sure I complete and understand all of the required tasks.
